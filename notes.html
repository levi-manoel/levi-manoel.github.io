First downside of Devenv

Don't get me wrong, Devenv is a big hand on the wheel, but today I encountered what, for me, is a downside. I'm working on a project that uses Bun on the backend, and yesterday (I think) Bun 1.2 was released. But since the default on the team is to use Devenv, and it uses Nix, I need to wait for Bun to be updated in nixpkgs.

Apparently the part of the text that relates to the title ends here, thanks for your attention.

This remember me a frustration I had these days. Normally, on my work notebook, I'm running NixOS, but one day I woke up and wanted to try something different, just to remember how a "normal system" works. So I went trying other distros.

First, I tried Debian. It was good; I installed i3 and set up my work environment without Devenv, and it was much nicer than I remember. But I couldn't easily install some newer versions of some packages, and that's okay; it's the idea of the distro. Since I was in a "free time, nothing better to do" mood, I went with Arch, btw. Me like Arch. I followed the installation guide, booted, didn't work. I refollowed the installation guide, then booted, and katchow, didn't work. Clearly a skill issue with partitioning the disk, but third time's the charm. I went with GNOME on Arch because I like GNOME. Set up my work environment, and again, a much better experience than I remember. But life is hard, and apparently, my notebook likes NixOS. Why?

The TypeScript LSP was very slow—20 seconds to show the suggestions of a type defined in the file, slow. Why not write code without autocomplete? I could give 200 reasons with another 100 reasons inside each one (database tables). And not only in Vscode, I used Neovim, also btw, and it was slow there too, I even installed a Neovim plugin that is faster that the LSP integration, it really was faster, like, 5 seconds faster. I tried to fix it; my friend tried to fix it; my friend who's my boss tried—no success. Last try to have a "normal" distro: Pop!_OS—not btw, I know. My previous everyday distro. I only switched from it because one day the kernel update broke my audio, and nothing fixed it. So, at the time, I tried NixOS (because a friend of mine talked a lot about it), and the TypeScript LSP was also slow.

So I came back to NixOS, and the LSP was fast again.
